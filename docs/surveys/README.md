# Surveys, Benchmarks & Analysis

## 📚 Overview

This category collects papers that do **not** propose a new reconstruction method. They
survey the field, benchmark existing models against each other, or probe what these
models have actually learned. They are kept separate because the standard paper template
assumes a method with its own results table, and applying that shape to a survey or a
benchmark produces a misleading document.

## 🎯 Why these matter for this collection

The feed-forward 3D reconstruction field moved from DUSt3R (CVPR 2024) to well over a
hundred derivative papers in roughly two years. Individual papers each claim SOTA on a
self-selected mix of benchmarks, which makes cross-paper comparison unreliable. The
papers here are the ones that step back:

- **Benchmarks** evaluate many models under one protocol, so the numbers are comparable.
- **Surveys** map the lineage and the design space.
- **Analysis papers** ask what the models learned rather than how well they score —
  e.g. whether epipolar geometry emerges without being supervised.

## ⚠️ Reading these critically

A survey is not automatically more reliable than the papers it summarizes. One entry in
this category is documented as containing factual errors about the models it
characterizes, and its venue carries little peer-review signal. Where such problems were
found during verification they are recorded in the paper's own document rather than
silently corrected — check the `Insights & Impact` section before relying on a claim.

Venue and metadata for every paper here were verified against primary sources
(arXiv, DBLP, OpenReview, CVF openaccess, or the authors' own project pages).
Where a venue could not be confirmed, the document says so instead of guessing.
