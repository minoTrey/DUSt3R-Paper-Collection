Figure 3. Overview of Flow3r. Flow3r predicts visual geometry using factored flow supervision, enabling scalable geometry learning
from unlabeled videos. Each input image is encoded and processed by the multi-view transformer to produce camera tokens and patch
tokens. For data with dense geometry and pose labels, we directly supervise the patch tokens and camera tokens with the corresponding
labels. For unlabeled datasets without geometry and pose supervision, we predict flow between two frames in a factorized manner,
supervised by the pseudo labels from an off-the-shelf 2D flow prediction model [46]. To obtain the factored flow, we fuse the patch
features of one frame with the camera features of another, and decode the fused representation through the DPT head to produce dense
flow predictions.
